Getting Started

First, a disclaimer. This is beginners advice from an hobbyist of over 10 years. All I can say has been said before, and better, by PADI, who should be your first stop with any queries.

Learn More at PADI.com

Advice

Equipment

BCD: A jacket that you can inflate and deflate with air to control your buoyancy. Also has hooks and straps to attatch the rest of your equipment to.

LPI: A connector from your BCD to your scuba tank which is used to inflate and deflate it.

Regulator and Octopus: Breathing implements. Regulator is your primary breather, a black cord. Octopus is a spare incase another divers regulator stops working, a yellow, and longer cord.

SPG: This gauge shows how much air you have remaining in your scuba tank.

Tips

  • You'll be paired up with another in a buddy system. Keep close to them.
  • Never hold your breath, particularly when ascending or descending. This is due to the air capacity in your lungs altering due to the difference in pressure. Simply breath normally.
  • Your primary way of controlling your bouyancy should be inflating and deflating your BCD, not by kicking your arms and legs.
  • While underwater, you are a guest. Don't take anything with you and keep fish disturbance to a minimum.
  • Avoid touching reefs, coral or rocks. If necessary, do so using your finger tips.
